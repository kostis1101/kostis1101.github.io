All Perfect Numbers are Odd-Square Free


By odd-square free one means that it is not divisible by an odd square.

Proof

Suppose \(A\) is a perfect number that is odd-square free, i.e. \(A = 2^n p_1 \cdot\cdot\cdot p_s\), with \(p1_, ..., p_s\) odd, pairwise distinct primes. Also suppose, without loss of generality, that \(p_1 < ... < p_s\).
Let \(\sigma(A)\) be the divisor sum of \(A\). Since \(A\) perfect, \(A = \sigma(A) - A\).

Now, \(p_s \mid A \ \Longrightarrow\ p_s \mid \sigma(A) - A \ \Longrightarrow\ p_s \mid \sigma(A)\). We also know that: \[ \sigma(A) = 2^{n + 1} (p_1 + 1) (p_2 + 1) \cdot \cdot \cdot (p_s + 1) \] exerciseSo, \(p_s \mid 2^{n + 1} (p_1 + 1) \cdot \cdot \cdot (p_s + 1)\). But, since \(p_s > p_i \ \forall i < s\), \(p_s \nmid (p_i + 1)\) and obviously \(p_s \nmid p_s + 1\). So, necessarily, \(p_s \mid 2^{n + 1}\). But: \[ 2A - p_1 \cdot\cdot\cdot p_s = 2^{n + 1} \ p_1 \cdot\cdot\cdot p_s - p_1 \cdot\cdot\cdot p_s = (2^{n + 1} - 1)p_1\cdot\cdot\cdot p_s \] And, since, \(p_s \mid 2^{m + 1} - 1\), \(p_s^2 \mid (2A - p1 \cdot\cdot\cdot p_s)\). Thus \(p_s^2 \mid 2A \ \Longrightarrow\ p_s^2 \mid A\), which is a contradiction.
