Every Markov Probability Matrix has a Positive Eigenvector using Convex Geometry

Proposition:

Let \(P\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix of non-negative entries with row vectors \((u_i)_{i \le n}\) such that \(\sum_j (u_i)_k = 1\) for all \(i\). Then, there exists an eigenvector \(v\) of non-negative coordinates such that \(v = v P\) and \(\sum_j v_j = 1\).

proof

Let \(\mathcal{T}: v \mapsto v P\) be the transformation restricted to the simplex \(S = \{x \in \mathbb R^n | \sum_i x_i = 1 \land x_i \ge 0\}\). \(\mathcal T\) maps \(S\) to some simplex \(A = \mathcal T [S] \subseteq S\). Also, \(\mathcal T\) is affine and thus continuous. By the Brouwer fixed point theorem there exists some \(v \in S\) such that \(v = \mathcal T(v) = v P\).
